    01. Problem & Main task

    Acknowledging the problem

    Connor is a singer and songwriter mainly focusing on expressing himself through his work and passion – indie rock music. People around him and himself perceive him as optimistic dreamer and believer in the power of nature.

    At the moment, his only way of reaching out to people and promoting himself is through social media platforms. Although they have their advantages, they come with many limitations.

    The focus of the project was to develop a website for Connor to represent himself as an artist, advertise his albums, concerts and singles and tell his full story in a way that inspires other people.

    02. Project planning

    Gathering the information and planning the project

    2.0. Meeting with the client

    Meeting Connor was a big step forwards for understanding him as a person and musician. We gathered key insights that helped us further in the brainstorming session for generating ideas on the look & feel of the website.

    2.1. Planning the project

    In the planning stage, we created two main things – work breakdown structure and to-do calendar. This helped us to oversee deadlines for important tasks as well as the overall project process. Our approach for the project was to assign main responsibilities for each of the members and define overall tasks that would be done together.

    Afterwards, we would meet as often as possible for the overall tasks and work independently on the personal ones.

    03. User research

    Analyzing the potential user

    3.0. Target audience and user categorization

    Before creating the user categorization and finding the best fit target audience for qualitative interviews, we had received crucial data from the clients Spotify, which helped us to move forward more efficiently and understand his listeners better.

    After analyzing the data, we conducted the user categorization using Kottler’s Marketing segmentation scheme, were we categorized users by 4 main segmentations.

    Main user categories

    Geographics

    International. According to the data, most of Connors listeners are from Agentina, however, his music - in English and Spanish.

    Demographics

    People between 20-40 years old, all genders, Spanish and English speaking.

    Occasions

    Walking, driving a car, relaxing, doing chores.

    Benefits

    Inspiration, calmness, relaxing mood, discovering new music.

    Psychographics

    Self reflective, eager to discover new music, dreamer, open-minded personality. Motivated to relax, change the mood, gain new cultural experiences through music.

    3.1. Interviews & analysis

    After categorizing the potential user, we developed a purpose of the interviews and research question, followed by the interview guide. After these steps, each of the group members conducted a qualitative interview. To understand Connor even better, we conducted a group interview with Connors closest people, which, as expected provided us with interesting and helpful insights.

    Main takeaways from individual interviews

    Purpose of listening

    Music is about emotions and feelings; lyrics and melody help people to connect with the artist.

    Finding artists

    Internet portals and social media is the most used platforms for finding artists, concerts and music overall.

    Occasions

    People usually listen to music on the way, doing every day chores or relaxing.

    Main takeaways from group interviews

    Connors personality

    Connor is passionate, enthusiastic, sensitive, determined, chill, talkative and curious person. People closest to him relate him to nature and sun in particular.

    Opportunities of growth

    For Connor to grow, he needs to find professional connections, which confirmed out previously formulated problem description.

    04. Ideations & Content

    Brainstorming, Priority matrix and Sitemaps

    4.0. Brainstorming session - Vicky

    As a starting point for solving the problem, we went through the analyzed data and performed brainstorming session with the use of VICKY method, generating ideas for content.

    4.1. Priority matrix

    After finishing the idea generation process, the importance of each idea and its execution difficulty were discussed and placed in the prioritization matrix figure. Since the number of the concepts was fairly large, we decided to eliminate some of them, eventually color coding the cards to emphasize the selected ones.

    4.2. Sitemaps & Information architecture

    The next step in the overall design process was determining the whole websites IA. The client meeting and qualitative research provided the base for the group to create. The creation of sitemap was an iterative process, in total with 2 different versions.

    05. Sketches & Lo-Fi wireframes

    Multiple iterations of sketches and wireframes

    5.1. Sketching process

    In the sketching stage, we gathered together and in a structured way, tried to put the content created in sitemaps, on the paper. Each of the group members drew multiple sketches, later on discussed in the group, the better ones were selected, adjusted and sketched again, until we came up with the final version of the sketching stage.

    5.1. Low fidelity wireframes

    Based on the previously made sketches, we designed first iterations of low-fidelity design. After completing this step, to gain additional feedback, they were shown to the client. The most problematic part was the about me page, as Connor’s intentions were unclear at first, however, after multiple iterations on the page, we came up with the final version of the wireframes, which allowed us to move forwards to the high-fidelity design stage.

    06. Design & Final product

    User tests and final design

    6.1. Color palette & font family

    Based on previously gathered data from interviews, Connor himself and his closest people, we decided to go with a monochromatic and warm color palette, that in our opinion fits his personality the best. Afterwards, we chose the font family, that is easily readable, modern and fits well together with indie-rock style.

    First iteration & user tests

    The first iteration of the high-fidelity design was made based on the initial wireframes, applying visual hierarchy using colors, font weights and different element sizes. During the designing, we made some minor changes for the homepage’s header, as we felt it did not fit well together with the overall looks of the website.

    5 Second test

    To get more thoughts and data on the first iteration of the design, we performed 5 second and think aloud tests, as well as got feedback from the client himself. During the 5 second test, we asked people questions, like, what they noticed first when they entered the website or if they remember anything of what was written in the photo, as well as if they noticed specific buttons. The test showed us, that we need to take steps back and re-think some of the parts, which later on were adjusted in the version 2.

    Think aloud test

    The purpose of the think aloud test was to discover any possible faults and ensure the navigation design allows users to find the desired information within a relatively short period of time. We had 4 participants and 5 tasks for each one of them. The results of the user tests showed that there are no major problems in the navigation design and is easy to find information and navigate throughout the website.

    6.2. The final design & Implementation

    Thanks to the conducting the tests and some minor changes, we ensured that the website’s information architecture and design is clear, meeting our expectations and goals, which lead us to finalizing the websites interface and finishing the design stage.

    After the design stage was ready to be developed, we divided the coding tasks by pages and began the development stage. The development was done using HTML, CSS and JavaScript technologies, followed up by optimizing SEO by maintaining a good semantic structure, creating schema markup and setting up open graph protocol.
